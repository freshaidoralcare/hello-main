What’s the Best Toothpaste for Kids?

written by TheFresh-Aid Team under
 category _category

It’s every parents’ happiness is to see a glimpse of that tiny tooth popping out. 

Over the next few months, that tiny tooth will eventually become two rows of teeth. 

And that’s when trouble starts.

Since your babies or your toddlers cannot do it for themselves yet, you have to do everything possible to keep their teeth clean and healthy. 

Their teeth, after all, will help them with chewing and speaking. 

So when do you start dental care for your babies and toddlers?

How do you take care of your baby’s teeth?

More importantly, what’s the best toothpaste for kids to prevent cavities?

When Do Baby Teeth Start to Appear

Baby teeth begin to appear during the first 6 to 10 months. In some cases, it appears as early as three months. And in very rare cases, some have a full set of teeth when they are born. 

In some instances also, children’s teeth do not appear early. It may take 12 months to more than a year before you see one. 

So basically, children get their teeth at different times. 

Baby teeth can arrive in any order. But for most, the center bottom teeth do arrive first. All baby teeth will become complete by the time your child is at 3 years old. 

When your child reaches 6 onwards, that’s when the adult teeth start to replace the baby teeth.

When to Start Caring for Your Baby’s Teeth and Gums

A baby’s teeth form while still in the womb. So they have actually a full set of teeth hiding inside their gums. 

So when should you start caring for your baby’s teeth?

Right away.

Starting with the gums.

Obviously, it won’t involve a toothbrush and toothpaste yet. All you need is a soft washcloth or piece of gauze to get started.

Take the following as first steps:

Step 1: Moistened the soft washcloth or piece of gauze
Step 2: Gently wipe your baby’s gum at least twice a day or after feeding or weening and before bedtime

The purpose is to prevent bacteria build-up inside the mouth and from clinging to your baby’s gums. This sticky film could also accumulate on your baby’s gums that damage your baby’s teeth as they come out. 

Do not forget the tongue as well. It also accumulates food and particles so you need to clean it together with the gums.

When Brushing Your Baby’s Teeth Starts

When your baby’s tooth starts to pop up one by one, you need to be prepared. This is when brushing starts. 

Some parents would still use cloth and that’s totally okay. But when you are using a toothbrush keep in mind the following: 

  • Look for a soft-bristled toothbrush
  • Find something with a small head
  • Have a toothbrush with a large handle

For a toothpaste, check out one with all-natural or organic ingredients like Fresh Aid Teeth and Gum Care.

It has no fluoride, SLS or any other synthetic ingredients that are harmful to your child’s health. 

Its all-natural ingredients fight cavities and bad breath addresses mainly the root cause of oral health problems. 

It’s a good first step to preventing tooth decay and gum problems. 

What are the steps to brushing your child’s teeth?

Step 1: Wet the toothbrush. You can use warm water to start off. When your child starts eating solid food, that when you can add a toothpaste.
Step 2: Start using a very small amount of toothpaste, if possible start with an amount similar to a grain of rice.  Gradually increase this to a pea-size when your child reaches the age of 3. 
Step 3: Brush gently your baby’s teeth, front and back. Make sure to also clean your baby’s tongue.

A reminder to parents is to brush their child’s teeth until he or she is capable of doing it on their own. 

You need to supervise them also until they learn to rinse, gurgle and spit without any help from you.

You also need to monitor your child’s teeth. Be on the lookout for any signs of plaque or tooth decay. Check if there are signs of brown or white spots on their teeth.

If you notice any of these signs, go to the dentists for an oral examination or advice. Your child should be able to visit a dentist by the time he or she is at a year old.

Your dentist can give you information and advice on what to do for baby tooth care, child’s teething, use of fluoride or other natural toothpaste, thumbsucking or when your child is sucking milk on a bottle still while sleeping. 

These are all important to protect your child’s teeth.
